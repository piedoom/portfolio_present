The Problem

Companies that create digital instruments often implement poor & impersonal user experiences.

The Solution

Create a modern and approachable audio software company that puts users' needs
first. Sales follow customer happiness.

I identified multiple problems with current solutions relating to both audio companies and marketplaces in general.

- Audio companies are still very proprietary, closed, and impersonal. Their web presence is usually sterile and overly professional for products like music.

- I also sought to create this site because current solutions weren't specialized enough, and took too much profit. One-off marketplace sites like Sellfy didn't allow for much branding work, and took as much as 20% of profits.

----

- the solution was to create my own approachable audio software marketplace that had a focus on user-centric design.

- Additionally, by creating my own site I could integrate another payment processor and bring fees down to as little as 3%.

User Journey

A digital commerce site isn't exactly groundbreaking territory - but it's easy to get things wrong.

My goal is to create as little friction as possible and encourage the user to purchase items.

I started by creating a user journey. I created a simple chart illustrating user navigation.

I highlighted information, sound samples, videos, and imagery since visuals are much more impactful than text. While a commerce site doesn't seem particularly exciting, there are a lot of common pitfalls I see in other music sites.

User Journey - Login

User sign-in pages can be where many potential customers lose interest.

Let's have as little friction as possible.

For instance, many sites will require a login before an item can be added to a user cart. Asking a user to login creates unnecessary friction that may cause them to leave.

Instead, I designed the site to get users invested before springing a login or credit card request. Any user - logged in or not - can wishlist items. That investment will later on hopefully encourage them to go the extra step to create an account or enter in payment information.

On the product side of the user experience, I made sure to list free resources and sound packs to encourage new users to register an account before even thinking about putting in credit card information.
After planning out the user journey, I created some quick lofi wireframes to visualize the website flow.

I'll go into greater detail about my process later in the presentation.
I then created a style guid for UI elements. I envisioned something clean, professional, light, and vibrant.
The title page was meant to introduce new users to what Resamplr is and what it provides. The extra image cue helps establish the brand as an audio comany.
Audio samples were especially important, so I created several variations before finding a layout that balanced visual design with just enough information.

I planned for the sound player to be persistent through page changes (similar to soundcloud) so the user could browse other products while listening to samples from another.
The product page shows all relevant information in a summary to the top right; the name, price, and file formats provided were clearly labeled. The middle column provided a more free-form layout.

Issues & Improvements

I created Resamplr in 2016 after learning some Rails & frontend code. However, I went back and reviewed my design in early 2018 as I learned a lot since my first iteration.

This next section will highlight the problems I found with my previous design, and what I did to resolve those issues.

New Guiding Principles

  1. Be Personal.
  2. Be Friendly.
  3. Be Modern.
  4. Be Dynamic.
Be Personal. Don't be corporate. Consumer should feel as though they always come first.

Be Friendly. Don't over-complicate interactions. Rewarding users with unique interactions is great, but it shouldn't be overdone.

Be Modern. Don't overuse imagery, borders, or other elements that can contribute to visual noise. This isn't a photography website, so make sure that images all contribute to the audio product being sold.

Be Dynamic. After all, we're artists.

The main objective is to provide a different and better experience for all users - an experience that is easier, more intuitive, cleaner, and more developer-friendly than competitors.
